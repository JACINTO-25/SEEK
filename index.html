Introduction

As the primary guides of our children, it is important that we are equipped with proper knowledge and understanding of topics related to their physical, emotional, and moral development. Today, sex education should no longer be considered a taboo subject, but rather a vital part of raising children to become safe, responsible, and respectful individuals.

This booklet provides information and suggestions on how to introduce and explain concepts related to sex education in ways that are age-appropriate and clear. Its goal is to strengthen the partnership between home and school, encouraging open and meaningful communication on this important topic.

This guide aims to support every parent and teacher in building a safer, more compassionate, and well-informed community for our youth.

Sa panahon ngayan, ang edukasyong sekswal ay hindi na dapat ituring na sensitibong usapin, kundi isang mahalagang bahagi ng paghubog sa kabataan upang sila ay maging ligtas, responsable, at may respeto sa sarili at sa iba.

Ang booklet na ito ay naglalaman ng mga impormasyon at mungkin kung paano maipapaliwanaq sa mga bata ang mga konserto ng edukasyong sekswal sa paraang angkop sa kanilang edad. Layunin nitong mapalakas ang ugnayan sa pagitan ng tahanan at paaralan upang mas maging bukas at epektibo ang komunikasyon ukol sa paksang ito.

Naway magsilibi itong tulong sa bawat magulang at guro upang makabuo tayo ng isang mas ligtas, mapgmalasaki, at maalam na komunidad para sa ating mga kabataan.

Objectives

This booklet is created to support both parents and teachers in teaching children about sex education in a way that is appropriate, safe, and empowering. It outlines shared goals to ensure a unified approach in guiding children's learning.

Objectives:

  1. Help parents and teachers introduce basic sex education concepts such as body awareness, consent, and personal safety in an age-appropriate and respectful way.
  2. Provide engaging and interactive activities that support children's understanding of their bodies and boundaries.
  3. Strengthen the partnership between home and school in building a safe, informed, and supportive environment for children.

Ang booklet na ito ay ginawa upang suportahan ang mga magulang at guro sa pagtuturo ng edukasyong sekswal sa mga bata sa paraang ligtas, akma sa kanilang edad, at nagbibigay ng kapangyarihan sa sarili. Nagtatakda ito ng mga layunin upang maging magkatuwang sa paggabay sa pagkatuto ng bata.

Mga Layunin:

  1. Tulungan ang mga magulang at guro na ipakilala ang mga pangunahing konsepto ng edukasyong sekswal tulad ng kamalayan sa katawan, pahintulot, at personal na kaligtasan sa paraang akma sa edad at may respeto.
  2. Magbigay ng mga kawili-wili at interaktibong aktibidad na susuporta sa pag-unawa ng mga bata sa kanilang katawan at hangganan.
  3. Palakasin ang ugnayan ng tahanan at paaralan sa pagbibigay ng ligtas, maalam, at suportadong kapaligiran para sa mga bata.

Know the Facts

GLOBAL Statistics on Sexual Abuse - according to UNICEF (2024)

  • 370 million girls and women globally—approximately 1 in 8—have experienced rape or sexual assault before the age of 18.
  • 240 to 310 million boys and men—about 1 in 11—have experienced rape or sexual assault during childhood.
  • 1 in 3 women globally have been subjected to physical and/or sexual violence by an intimate partner or non-partner.
  • 300 million children worldwide are victims of online sexual exploitation and abuse annually.

SEXUAL ABUSE IN THE PHILIPPINES - according to UNICEF PHILIPPINES (2023)

  • 7 million children in the Philippines are reportedly subjected to sexual and physical abuse each year.
  • In 2021, around 2 million Filipino children were subjected to online sexual abuse and exploitation.
  • Notably, 98% of rape victims are women, and incest accounts for 33% of reported cases.

What Do We Mean by "Sex Education"? | Ano nga ba ang "Edukasyong Sekswal"?

Let's clear something up: Sex education for children does not mean teaching explicit sexual content.

Instead, it includes:

  • Teaching the correct names for body parts
  • Helping children understand which touches are okay and which aren't
  • Teaching them how to say "no" and respect others' "no"
  • Preparing them for body changes during puberty
  • Discussing how to build respectful friendships and handle peer pressure
  • Teaching the basics of reproduction in a way they can understand

Linawin natin: Ang edukasyong sekswal para sa mga bata ay hindi nangangahulugang pagtuturo ng hayagang sekswal na nilalaman.

Sa halip, ito ay:

  • Pagtuturo ng tamang pangalan ng bahagi ng katawan
  • Pagpapaintindi kung anong uri ng paghipo ang tama at hindi tama
  • Pagtuturo kung paano magsabi ng "hindi" at igalang ang "hindi" ng iba
  • Paghahanda sa mga pagbabagong dulot ng pagdadalaga o pagbibinata
  • Pag-uusap tungkol sa pagkakaibigan, respeto, at tamang pakikitungo
  • Maingat at wastong pagpapaliwanag tungkol sa kung saan nanggagaling ang sanggol

Why early Sex Education Matters? | Bakit Mahalaga ang Maagang Edukasyong Sekswal?

Children are naturally curious. By the age of 6 or 7, many begin to ask questions about their bodies, where babies come from, and differences between boys and girls. If adults ignore these questions or avoid the topic, children may turn to unreliable sources like social media or peers.

Early sex education teaches:

  • Respect for one's body
  • Understanding of boundaries
  • Accurate names for body parts
  • How to stay safe at home, in school, and online

Madalas na mausisa ang mga bata. Sa edad na anim o pito, nagsisimula na silang magtanong tungkol sa kanilang katawan, kung saan nanggagaling ang mga sanggol, at ang pagkakaiba ng babae at lalaki. Kapag iniiwasan ng matatanda ang mga tanong na ito, maaaring kumuha ng maling impormasyon ang bata mula sa media o kaibigan.

Ang maagang edukasyong sekswal ay nagtuturo ng:

  • Paggalang sa sariling katawan
  • Pag-unawa sa personal na hangganan
  • Wastong pangaian ng bahagi ng katawan
  • Paraan ng pananatiling ligtas sa bahay, paraalan, at internet

Myths vs Facts | Mito at Katotohanan

Myth: Talking about sex encourages early sex.

Fact: Proper education delays early activity and helps make informed decisions.

Mito: Ang pag-uusap tungkol sa sex ay nagtutulak sa mga bata na mag-sex ng maaga.

Katotohanan: Ang tamang edukasyon ay nagpapaliban sa maagang pagkakaroon ng karanasan at tumutulong sa paggawa ng mga tamang desisyon.

Author's Note

As adults—whether we are parents, guardians, or teachers—we carry the responsibility of guiding children not just academically, but emotionally, socially, and personally. Talking about sex education is not about encouraging children to grow up too fast—it's about giving them the tools to protect themselves, to know their rights, and to value their bodies.

Sex education helps children understand the names and functions of their body parts, recognize safe and unsafe touch, learn about personal boundaries, and develop respect for themselves and others. When we teach these concepts early and with compassion, we are not taking away their innocence—we are strengthening it with truth and protection.

Let us work hand-in-hand to build a generation that is informed, confident, and respectful. The conversations may feel awkward at times, but silence can be more dangerous than discomfort. By being open, honest, and age-appropriate in our approach, we create a safe space for children to ask questions, express feelings, and grow up with dignity and awareness. Remember: a child who is informed is a child who is empowered.

Bilang mga matatanda—mga magulang, tagapag-alaga, o guro—tungkulin nating gabayan ang mga bata hindi lamang sa akademikong aspeto kundi pati na rin sa emosyonal, panlipunan, at personal na pag-unlad. Ang edukasyong sekswal ay hindi tungkol sa pagpapabilis ng kanilang paglaki, kundi sa pagbibigay ng kakayahan upang maprotektahan ang sarili, maunawaan ang kanilang mga karapatan, at pahalagahan ang kanilang katawan.

Tandaan: ang batang may tamang kaalaman ay batang may kapanayarthang protektahan ang sarili.

-Author
